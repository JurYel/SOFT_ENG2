The collected data contains outliers in the Gross Income as there are movies that generated large amounts of gross income from the populace. The normal distribution curve of the data presents a skewed distribution hence Log Transformation was used to resolve this issue to attain a visible correlation between Gross Income and Votes.


From the Scatter Plot above, if you draw a straight line along the collected dots you will find that the line will be slightly diagonal and tilted to the left, this representation shows the weak correlation where the linear association between the two variables are hardly observable. For a better view on the correlation, we used the Pearson Correlation formula to compute for the Correlation Coefficient which will determine the strength of linear relationship between the two quantitative variables.


The Pearson Correlation results to a 0.44 correlation coefficient which is the same as what was shown in scatter plot. This only shows a weak correlation between Gross Income and Votes. Therefore, in conclusion, Gross Income is dependent to the Votes and is directly proportional to each other. Most movie hosting or streaming sites are based on the votes a movie has for it to come out at the front page so the more votes a movie has then the more views it accumulates thus generating higher gross income.
